About the service

This service is for voluntary, community and other local organisations or groups to make a request for support, after exploring all local options and not finding the required support.

Service descriptors

Here are definitions of what we mean by the following terms, when we use them to describe this new service.

Service

Request support as a voluntary or community organisation’ service. A new service offered by the VSC Emergencies Partnership. Its purpose is to fill any gaps in local capacity to meet the needs of local organisations, during times of emergency.

Request

Details from a local organisation about support they need, communicated through the webform and emails following on from that.

Local organisation

A small-scale, community, voluntary or grassroots organisation that exists to help provide resources or expertise to answer local need.

Support

Resources, expert advice and guidance or information to meet a local need.

Brokering

Assigning a third party organisation, found regionally, to provide or organise the support requested for a local need.

Local need

Lack of resource, expertise or information at a local organisation. Examples: not enough DBS-checked volunteers, not knowing which funding schemes the organisation is eligible for.

Unmet need

The ‘Request support as a voluntary or community organisation’ service has not been able to broker the need at a regional level and has escalated it to national level.

Liaison lead

Local community and voluntary sector member, who also may be a local organisation leader, who has taken on a local need request and provision coordination role in our service.

Information manager

Manages information relating to local organisation requests, regionally.

Multiagency cell

A regional team of information managers including a duty manager, who meet regularly to assign regional brokers to support requests for local needs.

National level

Where unmet needs are pushed after they are not met regionally. They are submitted to the Strategic Coordination Group (SCG).
