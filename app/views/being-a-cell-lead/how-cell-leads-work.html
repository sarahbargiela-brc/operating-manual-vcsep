How cell leads work

Cell leads are responsible for chairing and convening multiagency cell (MAC) meetings in their region:

Reporting to:Strategic Coordination Group

Matrix reports (during a crisis):Broker, Information Manager, Community Engagement Mangers

Cell leads:

  • ensure that the brokering meetings are efficient, and outcome focused
  • ensure collaborative working across the sector within the tactical cell to meet unmet need.
  • ensure that Multi-agency cells are representative of the communities they are representing and operating with and the cell has appropriate broker capability to meet requests.
  • decide which organisation is best placed to fulfil the request, where consensus cannot be reached during brokering.
  • ensure partner organisations that accept a brokered request, confirm it can be fulfilled, and where necessary re-broker in the cell.
  • ensure risk, actions, issues and decisions logs are maintained.
  • are responsible for ensuring the standing up/down procedures are managed promptly.
  • are responsible for calling ad hoc urgent meetings when required.

Monitoring responsibilities:

  • ensure that Stakeholder Engagement Managers are working closely with Liaison Leads and Local Resilience Forums to provide the best supporting network to the Multi-agency cell.
  • escalate requests which cannot be met within the tactical cell to Strategic Coordination Group.
  • ensure that all Multi-agency cell members are compliant with IG and training requirements when onboarded by the Stakeholder Engagement Managers.
