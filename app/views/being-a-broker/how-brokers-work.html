How information managers work

An information manager will:

  • Produce daily reports to be sent on to liaison leads for triage.
  • Be the main point of contact for the liaison leads in their region regarding status of support requests.
  • Communicate with brokers to give access to data they require to be able to action requests they have agreed to support with.
  • Identify and report local insights and intelligence gathered from the ‘Request support as a local organisation’ service form to a regional and a national level for advocacy.
  • Support the regional tactical cell with information management, data collection, analysis, and visualisation.
  • Make sure information management sources and products are accurate, comprehensible, clear and simple.
  • Identify the potential risks, harm and opportunities related to the collection of data for the VCS EP ‘Request support as a local organisation’ service.
  • Act as a controller to the data collected from the VCS EP ‘Request support as a local organisation’ service at a regional level, providing and removing access to the data where necessary.
  • Represent liaison leads and present local organisations' information at the regional tactical cell meetings.
  • Capture and feed back lessons learnt to the VCS EP members for future preparedness and response activities.
